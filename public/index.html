    Multidimensional Scaling

    Multidimensional scaling (MDS) is an umbrella term for several methods that operate on dissimilarities. Euclidean distances are examples of dissimilarities but dissimilarities are more general in that they can be any kind of measure of difference between two data items. The goal of MDS is find a configuration of points in the plane, or more generally the Euclidean space, so that their distances well represent the original dissimilarities.
